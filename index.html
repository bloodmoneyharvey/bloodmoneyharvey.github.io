About Bloodmoney Harvey

Bloodmoney Harvey is a unique clicker game that combines psychological horror and dark humor. The game was developed by Shroomychrist Studios and released in August 2025. Bloodmoney Harvey challenges players with moral choices in a life-or-death situation! You need $25,000 to pay for a life-saving surgery, and Harvey Harvington, a mysterious stranger, pays for each click, but at the cost of his pain. With adorable pastel visuals that contrast with the dark theme, the game offers a short but profound experience of about 30 minutes, making you reflect on the value of money and humanity.

Bloodmoney Harvey Story and Setting

In Bloodmoney Harvey, you play as a desperate character, facing financial pressure to save the life of yourself or a loved one. Harvey Harvington appears as a charming stranger who offers to pay him $1 for each click, but each action causes him damage ranging from mild discomfort to excruciating pain. The game’s premise is simple, focusing only on Harvey and the money you earn, but is enhanced by the pastel art style and the original “PAID IN BLOOD” soundtrack. How far will you go to achieve your goal, and can your conscience bear the cost? Three endings—Survival, Corruption, and Failure—make each decision deeply meaningful.

Gameplay and Moral Choices

Bloodmoney Harvey uses a simple yet emotional click mechanic. Each click on Harvey earns you $1, and you can buy tools from the shop to increase your income, from harmless feathers to scary things like guns. Each tool not only increases the amount of money but also changes Harvey’s state of mind, from a friendly smile to a pained, even desperate expression. The game does not have traditional levels but focuses on interactive moments, where each choice leads you to one of three outcomes. The combination of clicking and morality makes the game an addictive and haunting experience entirely different from the typical clicker games.

Bloodmoney Harvey's Tips for an Optimized Experience

  • Experiment with tools: Buy tools like feathers, needles, or guns to see how they affect the plot. Powerful tools often lead to the Corruption Ending.
  • Watch for hidden signals: Changes in background colors, sounds, or Harvey's dialogue reveal important details about the story.
  • Play slowly at the beginning of the game: Don't rush to buy powerful tools right away; explore Harvey's reactions to better understand the plot.
  • Try multiple strategies: Replay with different approaches—like prioritizing safe tools or maximizing income—to unlock all three endings.
  • Pause to reflect: Before buying powerful tools, consider the consequences, as they not only affect gameplay but also make you question your morality.

Bloodmoney FAQs

Is it possible to complete the game without harming Harvey?

Yes, you can achieve the Survival Ending by using safe tools like feathers and playing slowly. However, this method takes longer and requires patience.

How many endings are there in Blood Money, Harvey?

The game has three main endings: Survival Ending (causing less harm), Corruption Ending (maximizing profits with powerful tools), and Failure Ending (failing to reach financial goals). Additionally, there are slight variations in dialogue depending on choices.

Does Harvey change every time you play?

Yes, Harvey's behavior changes based on the tools you use. While he still makes small changes in the safe playthrough, powerful tools will make him more painful and haunting, especially in the Corruption Ending.

Is it possible to change strategies mid-game?

Yes, you can change tools mid-game, but your initial choices still affect the ending. For example, using the power tool early can lock you into the Corruption Ending.

Is the game suitable for all ages?

Bloodmoney Harvey contains psychological themes and mild horror, making it more suitable for players 13 and up (rated T for Teen). Parents are advised to review the content due to its sensitive moral nature.

Where to play BloodMoney online?

You can play it directly on your browser or PC at https://bloodmoneyharvey.github.io. There is no official mobile version yet, but the game is optimized for both computers and mobile devices.
